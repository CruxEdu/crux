Class 9: The Integrated Core

A revised, application-based curriculum framework focusing on functional intelligence.

1. Civic Literacy & Practical Governance

Focus: Real-world systems, legal rights, and structural awareness.
  • The Justice System: Understanding fundamental legal rights, consumer courts, and the practical steps to file a First Information Report (FIR).
  • Economic Citizenship: Introduction to taxation frameworks, direct vs. indirect taxes, and how municipal budgets are allocated.
  • Local Governance: Active case studies on municipal corporations, panchayats, and how a citizen can lodge civic complaints effectively.

2. Media & Information Literacy (MIL)

Focus: Critical thinking, verification, and digital safety.
  • Information Architecture: Decoding social media algorithms, confirmation bias, and the anatomy of "fake news" and misinformation.
  • Data Privacy: Understanding digital footprints, personal data protection, encryption basics, and avoiding online financial fraud.
  • Source Verification: Practical lab sessions on reverse-image searching, cross-referencing metadata, and identifying deepfakes.

3. Mental Health, Biology & Wellness

Focus: Emotional intelligence integrated with physiological science.
  • The Biological Machine: Human anatomy, metabolic health, nutrition engineering, and understanding lifestyle diseases over textbook memorization.
  • Emotional Mechanics: Scientific breakdown of stress, anxiety, neurotransmitters, and actionable frameworks for emotional regulation and boundary setting.
  • Reproductive Health: Evidence-based, gender-neutral education covering physiological changes, consent, and bodily autonomy.

4. Applied Philosophy & Cognitive Logic

Focus: Developing rational thinking models rather than rote conclusions.
  • Logical Fallacies: Identifying cognitive biases, ad hominem attacks, strawman arguments, and correlation vs. causation in public discourse.
  • Philosophical Frameworks: Core introductory concepts from Indian Nyaya logic, Socratic questioning, and Stoicism applied to modern decision-making.
  • Ethics in Action: Debating real-world ethical dilemmas (e.g., AI ethics, environmental triage) using structured logic.
